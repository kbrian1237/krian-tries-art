WHAT IS ART BY BRIAN KATHURIMA

Art, the splash of colors and the stroke of brushes, has been tickling the human fancy since the dawn of time. It's the silent language that speaks volumes, the visual melody that sings to our souls. From the cave paintings of Lascaux to the abstract expressions of Jackson Pollock, art has been the playground for human emotion and intellect. It's like a time machine, capturing the essence of each era, each brushstroke a footprint in the sands of time.

But let's not forget the whimsical side of art. Imagine if Picasso had been a comedian, his paintings might have been the punchlines to the world's most visual jokes. Or if Van Gogh had been into selfies, the 'Starry Night' might have had a cheeky photobomb by the artist himself. Art can be as playful as a kitten chasing a laser pointer, each movement unpredictable yet full of grace.

Then there's the debate about what constitutes 'real' art. Is a banana taped to a wall art? Well, if it makes you stop, stare, and stroke your chin in deep thought, perhaps it's done its job. Art doesn't have to be serious; it can be as serious as a cat in a tiny hat. It's the expression that counts, the ability to evoke emotion, whether it's awe or a hearty chuckle.

Of course, art isn't just confined to galleries and museums; it's all around us. Street art, for instance, turns our urban jungles into open-air galleries. Here, graffiti isn't just vandalism; it's a voice for the voiceless, a splash of rebellion on a concrete canvas. It's the art of the people, for the people, and by the people, and sometimes it even comes with the thrill of not getting caught.

Lastly, let's muse on the future of art. With digital technology, art is no longer limited to tangible mediums. It's becoming as boundless as the internet itself. Virtual reality art installations could mean swimming through a sea of Van Gogh's sunflowers or walking on the moon with Dali's melting clocks. The possibilities are as endless as the imagination, proving that art, like a good meme, will always find a way to evolve and capture our hearts.
